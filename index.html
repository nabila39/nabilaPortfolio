About Me

Hello! I’m Nabila Taha, a passionate data science and analytics enthusiast on a mission to transform raw data into valuable insights. With a strong academic background in computer science from Birzeit University, I’ve built a solid foundation in programming, data analysis, and machine learning, which fuels my continuous drive to explore the vast world of data.

My journey began with a deep curiosity to understand the stories hidden within data, and it has since evolved into a commitment to harness data-driven insights for impactful decision-making. As an active learner on DataCamp, I constantly expand my skills through hands-on projects, delving into real-world challenges and applying cutting-edge techniques.

I’ve tackled diverse projects, including analyzing TV viewership trends, investigating patterns in Netflix movies, predicting smoking status, and assessing diabetes risk using predictive models. My capstone project in deep learning involved training a model capable of identifying biases in sentences, particularly those targeting Palestinians or Israelis, demonstrating my interest in socially impactful technology applications.

In addition to data science, I am deeply interested in web development, especially using React and vanilla JavaScript, where I enjoy crafting interactive and responsive user interfaces. I am also keenly interested in API design and development, leveraging frameworks like Django and Spring Boot to create robust and scalable backend systems. This blend of frontend and backend skills allows me to build full-stack applications that bring data-driven solutions to life.

Beyond the world of tech, I enjoy swimming and drawing, which help me stay balanced and creative. As I continue to grow in the field, I am eager to contribute my knowledge, collaborate with fellow data enthusiasts, and make meaningful contributions to the data science and development communities.
